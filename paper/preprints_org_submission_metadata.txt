TurboPress: A Measurement-Driven Study of Rotated Low-Bit Weight Quantization — Equilibration Exponents, Error Feedback, and Per-Layer Bit Allocation

--- ABSTRACT (plain text) ----------------------------------------------
We present TurboPress, an instrumented post-training quantization (PTQ) pipeline for large language model (LLM) weights, together with a set of controlled A/B measurements that isolate the contribution of each design choice at exactly matched bit budgets. Building on the incoherence-processing line of work (QuIP, QuIP#, QTIP, QuaRot) and on the rotation-plus-optimal-quantizer construction of TurboQuant, TurboPress combines a seeded randomized orthogonal rotation, trellis-coded quantization (TCQ) with data-free trellis-optimized codebooks, GPTQ/LDLQ error feedback, and per-layer bit allocation. Our contributions are primarily empirical and analytical rather than a claim of a new state of the art. First, we derive a corrected rotation-aware equilibration exponent: in the weight-only, post-rotation regime the optimal per-channel fold is a quarter power, s_j = (E[x_j^2] / ||W_col_j||^2)^(1/4), which is distinct from the square-root heuristic of AWQ/SmoothQuant and from the weight–activation balancing result of BASE-Q. Second, we report a rigorous negative result: unbiased (QJL-style) residual correction, though provably debiasing, loses to a finer deterministic quantizer at equal bit budget for weights, because the correction's variance exceeds the bias it removes. Third, in an end-to-end evaluation on Qwen3-0.6B we show that error feedback and trellis coding are complementary — trellis coding wins at 2 bits, while both are near-lossless at 4 bits — and that mixed precision allocated by measured KL sensitivity beats uniform precision at an equal 3.0-bit budget. All results use a KL-divergence "visible-loss" metric with exact storage accounting, and the code, configurations, and raw result logs are released. We deliberately quantify the scale (<= 0.6B parameters) and baseline gaps that separate these measurements from a state-of-the-art claim.
