Quantization today is vibes.

Teams spend months fine-tuning a model, then deploy a 4-bit file from a stranger’s Hugging Face page because serving fp16 costs 4–8× more. Nobody measures what the compression did. Nobody signs it. Nobody is accountable when it silently gets worse at the one task that matters.

Silent regressions

A 2-bit quant of a 1.7B model can score 99.999× worse perplexity than a validated one at the same size. Both files look identical in a folder.

One-size-fits-none

The right bit-width differs per layer and per target device. Uniform 4-bit wastes budget where the model is robust and starves it where it’s fragile.

No receipt

Procurement and compliance now ask: is the deployed model the one you evaluated? Today the honest answer is a shrug.

Push. Press. Prove.

One command in CI. Every fine-tune, every base-model bump, every target device.

Step 1 — Push

Connect your model

Point TurboPress at a Hugging Face repo or a checkpoint in your bucket. Llama, Qwen, and Mistral families supported out of the box.

Step 2 — Press

Compress to a matrix of targets

Rotation → corrected channel equalization → trellis-coded quantization with per-layer bit allocation. Export TurboPress, GGUF, or FP8 — tuned per device.

Step 3 — Prove

Get the certificate

Signed manifest with measured KL, agreement, and benchmark deltas against your full-precision model. Fails your pipeline if fidelity slips.

# .github/workflows/release.yml
- uses: godsonj64/turbopress/action@v1
  with:
    api-key: ${{ secrets.TURBOPRESS_API_KEY }}
    model: your-org/support-agent-8b
    targets: "4bit,3bit"
    gates: "mean_kl_max=0.10,top1_agreement_min=0.80"

Built on research we publish, including the failures.

The pipeline exists because we measured everything — and kept the results that hurt. Read the full write-up: the preprint and the open results.

The quarter-power rule

The industry-standard activation scaling (AWQ’s square root) is provably suboptimal under rotation — on Qwen3-1.7B it’s worse than nothing at 3–4 bits. We derived and validated the correct exponent.

Data-free codebooks

Rotation makes weight statistics known in advance, so our trellis codebooks are designed offline — we compress regulated models without ever seeing customer data.

A published negative result

We tested the celebrated unbiased-sketch correction for weights, found it loses to plain deterministic coding at equal budget, and published that too. You should trust vendors who show their dead ends.
